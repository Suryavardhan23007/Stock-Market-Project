# 365. MARKET MICROSTRUCTURE LAYER OVERVIEW

The Market Microstructure Layer is the deepest and most advanced market observation layer in the entire project.

While:

Price shows what happened.

Volume shows how much happened.

OI shows positioning.

News shows information flow.

FII/DII shows institutional capital flow.

The Microstructure Layer attempts to answer:

How is the market mechanically moving right now?

This layer observes the actual battle occurring between buyers and sellers.

---

# 366. WHY MICROSTRUCTURE EXISTS

Most retail traders only see:

OHLCV

Open

High

Low

Close

Volume

However:

Institutional participants see significantly more information.

Examples:

Bid Queue

Ask Queue

Order Book Depth

Spread Dynamics

Liquidity Shifts

Order Flow Imbalances

The Microstructure Layer attempts to capture these signals.

---

# 367. LONG-TERM OBJECTIVE

The objective is not:

Indicator Generation.

The objective is:

Understand the internal mechanics of price movement.

# 374. ORDER FLOW IMBALANCE (OFI)

One of the most important microstructure features.

Purpose:

Measure buyer pressure versus seller pressure.

---

# 375. OFI INTUITION

If buyers aggressively consume liquidity:

OFI becomes positive.

If sellers aggressively consume liquidity:

OFI becomes negative.

---

# 376. WHY OFI MATTERS

Price often reacts after:

Order flow changes.

OFI attempts to detect:

Pressure

before

Price Movement.

---

# 377. OFI AS A LEADING INDICATOR

Traditional indicators are:

Lagging.

OFI is closer to:

Real-time market intent.

---

# 378. MICROPRICE OVERVIEW

Microprice is a more advanced version of price.

Instead of observing:

Last Traded Price

it observes:

Liquidity-Weighted Price.

---

# 379. MICROPRICE FORMULA

Conceptually:

Microprice weights price by:

Bid Volume

Ask Volume

This produces a more informative estimate of:

Future price direction.

---

# 380. MICROPRICE PURPOSE

Detect hidden pressure inside the order book.

Example:

Price appears unchanged.

Microprice rises.

This suggests:

Buyers dominate liquidity.

---

# 381. WHY MICROPRICE IS USEFUL

Markets frequently move:

After

Microprice shifts.

Not before.

---

Risk

---

# 385. WIDE SPREAD

Execution Risk

# 388. QUEUE IMBALANCE

Purpose:

Compare:

Pending Buy Orders

versus

Pending Sell Orders.

---

# 389. QUEUE IMBALANCE INTERPRETATION

Large Bid Queue:

Potential Support.

Large Ask Queue:

Potential Resistance.

---

# 390. LIMITATION OF QUEUE ANALYSIS

Large participants can:

Cancel Orders.

Therefore:

Queue signals must be treated cautiously.

---

# 391. DEPTH ANALYSIS

The order book contains:

Multiple price levels.

Not just:

Best Bid

Best Ask.

Future versions may analyze:

Depth Distribution.

---

# 392. DEPTH WALLS

Large liquidity concentrations create:

Liquidity Walls.

These often influence:

Short-term price movement.

---

# 393. LIQUIDITY VACUUMS

Areas with little depth can produce:

Rapid price movement.

because:

Few orders exist to absorb pressure.

---

# 394. MARKET IMPACT

Purpose:

Estimate how much price moves when liquidity is consumed.

---

# 395. WHY MARKET IMPACT MATTERS

A market with:

Low liquidity

may move aggressively on small orders.

A highly liquid market behaves differently.
