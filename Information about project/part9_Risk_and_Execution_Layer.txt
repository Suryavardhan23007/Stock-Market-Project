# PART 9 — RISK MANAGEMENT, EXECUTION LAYER, POSITION SIZING, TRADE LIFECYCLE AND CAPITAL PRESERVATION

---

# 301. RISK & EXECUTION LAYER OVERVIEW

The Risk & Execution Layer is the final decision enforcement layer of the project.

Everything before this point is designed to answer:

"What should we do?"

The Risk & Execution Layer answers:

"How much should we do?"

and

"Should we do it at all?"

This distinction is critical.

Many trading systems fail not because their predictions are wrong.

They fail because:

Risk Management is poor.

---

# 302. PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITY

The Risk Layer is responsible for:

Position Sizing

Capital Allocation

Trade Validation

Trade Approval

Risk Control

Stop Loss Management

Take Profit Management

Execution Management

Portfolio Protection

---

# 303. CORE PHILOSOPHY

The project assumes:

Risk Management is more important than prediction.

A mediocre model with excellent risk management can survive.

An excellent model with poor risk management eventually blows up.

Therefore:

Risk is treated as a first-class component.

---

# 304. THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE

Protect Capital First.

Generate Alpha Second.

No trade is mandatory.

Survival is mandatory.

---

# 305. TRADE PIPELINE

The decision flow becomes:

Intelligence Layers
→

This is the final path before capital is exposed.

# 306. WHY THE RISK LAYER EXISTS

Suppose:

ML Model says BUY.

55%

10%

50%

100%

The ML model cannot answer this.

The Risk Layer must answer it.

# 307. POSITION SIZING OVERVIEW

Position sizing determines:

How much capital enters a trade.

This often has more impact on profitability than signal accuracy.

---

# 308. POSITION SIZING PRINCIPLE

The project does not use:

Fixed Capital Allocation.

Example:

Always invest ₹10,000.

This approach ignores:

Volatility

Confidence

Market Conditions

Risk

---

# 309. PRIMARY POSITION SIZING MODEL

Fractional Kelly Criterion

will be used.

---

# 310. WHY NOT FULL KELLY

Full Kelly is mathematically optimal.

However:

It is extremely aggressive.

Produces:

Large Drawdowns

High Emotional Stress

High Volatility of Equity Curve

Professional traders rarely use:

100% Kelly.

---

# 311. FRACTIONAL KELLY

Instead:

Fractional Kelly will be used.

Examples:

25% Kelly

50% Kelly

75% Kelly

The optimal fraction will be determined empirically.

---

# 312. KELLY INPUTS

The Kelly engine requires:

Probability of Success

Risk Reward Ratio

Expected Return

These inputs are supplied by:

Meta-Labeler

Risk Engine

Historical Validation

# 316. TRADE REJECTION SYSTEM

Not every signal deserves execution.

The Risk Layer has authority to:

Reject Trades.

---

# 317. WHY TRADE REJECTION EXISTS

A signal can be:

Directionally Correct

but

Operationally Dangerous.

Examples:

Extreme Volatility

Low Liquidity

Major News Event

Execution Risk

The trade should be blocked.
