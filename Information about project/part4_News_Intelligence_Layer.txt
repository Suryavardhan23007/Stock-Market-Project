# PART 4 — NEWS INTELLIGENCE LAYER, EVENT EXTRACTION, FINBERT INTEGRATION, IMPACT CALIBRATION AND NEWS FEATURE ENGINEERING

---

# 80. NEWS INTELLIGENCE LAYER OVERVIEW

The News Intelligence Layer is the third major intelligence layer of the project.

Purpose:

To transform unstructured market news into structured market intelligence.

The layer does NOT exist to summarize news.

The layer does NOT exist to create a chatbot.

The layer does NOT exist to generate news reports.

The layer exists to answer:

"What information entered the market?"

and

"How important is that information?"

---

# 81. THE BIGGEST MISTAKE IN NEWS ANALYSIS

Most retail systems perform:

Article
→
Sentiment
→
Prediction

This approach is fundamentally flawed.

Example:

Headline A:

"Tesla cuts Model 3 prices by 5%"

Headline B:

"Federal Reserve increases interest rates by 50bps"

FinBERT may assign similar negative sentiment values.

However:

The Tesla announcement affects a single company.

The Federal Reserve announcement affects:

Global Capital Flows

Currencies

Bond Markets

Equities

Commodities

Risk Appetite

These events are not equivalent.

Therefore:

Sentiment alone is insufficient.

---

# 82. CORE PHILOSOPHY OF THE NEWS LAYER

The purpose is not:

"Is this article positive?"

The purpose is:

"What market event does this article represent?"

The architecture therefore focuses on:

Event Intelligence

instead of

Sentiment Intelligence.

---

# 83. NEWS LAYER RESPONSIBILITIES

The News Intelligence Layer must answer:

What happened?

Who caused it?

Which market is affected?

Which sector is affected?

How important is it?

How unusual is it?

How many sources are reporting it?

Did institutions react?

How long should its influence persist?

---

# 84. NEWS COLLECTION PHILOSOPHY

The layer must collect:

Raw News

before

News Intelligence.

This follows the same philosophy used in:

OI Intelligence

and

FII/DII Intelligence.

Raw information is always preserved.

The archive must store:

Headline

URL

Metadata

This becomes the permanent news memory.

# 86. WHY RAW NEWS IS STORED

Without raw archives:

Therefore:

Raw news is never discarded.

# 89. NEWS CHRONOLOGY PRINCIPLE

News must obey strict chronology.

Example:

An article published:

Monday 8 PM

may influence:

Tuesday Market

Monday Market.

This rule is mandatory.

---

# 90. THREE-STAGE NEWS FILTERING PIPELINE

The News Layer processes information through multiple stages.

Purpose:

Prevent noise from reaching expensive NLP models.

This reduces:

Latency

Cost

False Signals

---

# 91. STAGE 1 — TOKEN MATCHER

Purpose:

Fast keyword filtering.

This stage uses:

Entity Dictionaries

Market Dictionaries

Economic Dictionaries

Ticker Dictionaries

Examples:

RBI

SEBI

Inflation

GDP

Repo Rate

NIFTY

BANKNIFTY

Reliance

HDFC

Federal Reserve

Crude Oil

USDINR

Only articles passing this stage proceed further.

---

# 92. WHY TOKEN MATCHING EXISTS

The internet contains enormous noise.

Examples:

Sports

Entertainment

Politics unrelated to markets

Lifestyle content

These should never consume FinBERT resources.

Token matching removes irrelevant content early.

---

# 93. STAGE 2 — MARKET RELEVANCE FILTER

Purpose:

Verify whether the article is genuinely market relevant.

This stage uses:

Sentence Embeddings

and

Similarity Analysis.

---

# 94. MARKET RELEVANCE CENTROID

A dedicated:

Market Relevance Centroid

is constructed.

Method:

Collect hundreds of historically important market articles.

Convert them into embeddings.

Average them.

Create a representative market vector.

---

# 95. LIVE RELEVANCE CHECK

Every incoming article:

Generates an embedding.

Cosine Similarity is calculated.

If similarity exceeds threshold:

Article proceeds.

Otherwise:

Article is discarded.

---

# 96. WHY THIS MATTERS

Keyword matching alone is insufficient.

Example:

An article mentioning:

"Bank"

does not automatically affect markets.

The relevance filter prevents false positives.

---

# 97. STAGE 3 — FINBERT

Purpose:

Extract financial sentiment.

Outputs:

Positive Probability

Negative Probability

Neutral Probability

Raw Sentiment Score

Formula:

S = Positive Probability − Negative Probability

---

# 98. WHY FINBERT IS NOT THE FINAL ANSWER

FinBERT measures:

Language Sentiment

It does NOT measure:

Economic Impact.

Therefore:

Additional calibration layers are required.

# 104. CALIBRATED SENTIMENT

Formula Concept:

Calibrated Sentiment

=

Raw FinBERT Sentiment

×

Entity Weight

Small news

and

Systemic news

# 105. NEWS VELOCITY

Measure information spread.

Formula Concept:

Number of unique articles

within a rolling time window.

---

# 106. WHY NEWS VELOCITY MATTERS

May be irrelevant.

Fifty simultaneous articles:

May indicate a systemic event.

News velocity measures:

Market attention.
