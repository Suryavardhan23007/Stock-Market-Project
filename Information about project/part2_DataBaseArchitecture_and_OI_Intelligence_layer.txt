# 19. OI INTELLIGENCE LAYER OVERVIEW

The OI Intelligence Layer is the first major intelligence layer built in this project.

Purpose:

To measure institutional positioning inside the derivatives market.

The layer attempts to answer:

"What are derivative participants doing?"

instead of:

"What is price doing?"

Price tells us:

What happened.

OI tells us:

Who is committing capital.

This distinction is extremely important.

---

# 20. WHY OI MATTERS

Open Interest measures:

Total outstanding derivative contracts.

When OI changes:

New risk enters or leaves the market.

Price alone cannot reveal this.

Examples:

Price Up + OI Up

Fresh bullish participation.

Price Up + OI Down

Short covering.

Price Down + OI Up

Fresh bearish positioning.

Price Down + OI Down

Long liquidation.

These behaviors frequently precede future market movement.

---

# 21. OI LAYER OBJECTIVE

The OI layer is designed to transform raw option chain snapshots into:

The system must answer:

Where are positions accumulating?

# 29. OI VELOCITY

Purpose:

Measure how quickly OI changes.

Formula Concept:

Current OI
minus
Previous OI

High velocity indicates:

Aggressive institutional positioning.

Low velocity indicates:

Passive market behavior.

---

# 30. OI ACCELERATION

Purpose:

Measure second derivative of OI.

This answers:

Is positioning speeding up?

Acceleration often matters more than velocity.

Many large market moves begin when OI acceleration spikes.

---

# 31. PUT CALL RATIO (PCR)

Purpose:

Measure aggregate market positioning.

Formula:

Total Put OI
/
Total Call OI

Interpretation:

High PCR

Defensive positioning.

Low PCR

Aggressive bullish positioning.

PCR alone is not predictive.

PCR must be combined with:

Price

OI Velocity

Regime State

---

# 32. ATM FLOW

Purpose:

Measure positioning around ATM strikes.

ATM behavior often contains the highest concentration of institutional positioning.

The goal:

Detect capital concentration near current price.

# 34. GAMMA EXPOSURE (GEX)

Purpose:

Estimate dealer hedging pressure.

High positive GEX:

Volatility suppression.

High negative GEX:

Volatility amplification.

This becomes critical during:

Breakouts

Crashes

Short squeezes

---

# 35. WALL DETECTION

Purpose:

Identify:

Call Walls

Put Walls

Call Wall:

Put Wall:

Strike with highest put concentration.

These often behave as:

Resistance

Support

respectively.
