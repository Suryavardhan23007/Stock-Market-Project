# 422. ALPHA DEFINITION

Alpha means:

Predictive Information

that improves trading performance.

Not:

Interesting Charts

---

# 423. WHY CORRELATION IS NOT ENOUGH

Many variables appear useful historically.

However:

Correlation does not imply predictive power.

Therefore:

Correlation alone is never sufficient.

---

# 424. INFORMATION COEFFICIENT (IC)

One of the primary validation metrics.

Purpose:

Measure predictive relationship between:

Feature

and

Future Return.

---

# 425. IC INTERPRETATION

Positive IC:

Feature has predictive value.

Negative IC:

Feature predicts opposite direction.

Near Zero:

Feature contains little information.

# 437. BACKTESTING OVERVIEW

Backtesting simulates:

Historical Trading.

Purpose:

Estimate real-world performance.

---

# 438. WHAT BACKTESTING SHOULD ANSWER

Would the strategy have worked?

How often?

How much risk?

How much drawdown?

How much return?

---

# 439. DANGER OF BACKTESTS

Most backtests are misleading.

Reasons:

Data Leakage

Survivorship Bias

Overfitting

Look-Ahead Bias

Selection Bias

---

# 440. CHRONOLOGY RULE

No backtest may access:

Future Information.

Ever.

This is non-negotiable.

---

# 441. LOOK-AHEAD BIAS

Example:

Using Tuesday data

to predict Tuesday.

This is forbidden.

All feature stores must use:

T-1 information only.

# 442. PURGED VALIDATION

# 443. EMBARGO PERIODS

# 444. WALK-FORWARD TESTING

# 445. WHY WALK-FORWARD EXISTS

# 457. SHARPE RATIO

Widely used.

# 458. WHY SHARPE IS NOT ENOUGH

Testing many models increases chance of:

False Discoveries.

Therefore:

Additional metrics are required.

---

# 459. DEFLATED SHARPE RATIO (DSR)

One of the most important metrics.

Purpose:

Correct for multiple testing.

---

# 460. WHY DSR EXISTS

If 1000 models are tested:

Some will appear profitable by luck.

DSR attempts to identify:

Real Alpha

vs

Random Noise.

# 461. NON-NEGOTIABLE RULE
