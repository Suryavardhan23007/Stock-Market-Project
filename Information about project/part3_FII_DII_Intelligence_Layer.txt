# 48. FII/DII INTELLIGENCE LAYER OVERVIEW

The FII/DII Intelligence Layer is the second major intelligence layer in the project.

Purpose:

To measure the behavior of institutional capital in the Indian equity market.

While the OI Layer focuses on derivative positioning, the FII/DII Layer focuses on actual capital movement.

The OI layer answers:

"What are derivative participants doing?"

The FII/DII layer answers:

"Where is institutional money flowing?"

These are related but fundamentally different questions.

Both layers are required.

---

# 49. WHY FII/DII DATA MATTERS

Markets are not moved primarily by retail traders.

Large movements are usually driven by:

Foreign Institutional Investors (FII/FPI)

and

Domestic Institutional Investors (DII)

because these entities control enormous pools of capital.

Examples:

Mutual Funds

Insurance Companies

Pension Funds

Sovereign Funds

Foreign Asset Managers

Global Hedge Funds

When these participants aggressively accumulate positions, market structure changes.

Therefore:

Institutional flows provide information that price alone cannot reveal.

---

# 50. CORE HYPOTHESIS OF THE FII/DII LAYER

The hypothesis is simple:

Persistent institutional buying or selling creates market pressure that can influence future returns.

Examples:

5-day FII Buying Streak

may indicate:

Sustained foreign capital inflows.

5-day FII Selling Streak

may indicate:

Persistent capital withdrawal.

Similarly:

FII and DII divergence can reveal market conflict.

Example:

FIIs aggressively selling

while

DIIs aggressively buying.

This often signals institutional disagreement.

Such disagreements can become useful predictive features.

Unlike OI data, FII/DII data is published daily.

The layer uses official NSE disclosures.

Empirical verification was performed before implementation.

The architecture was not built using assumptions.

The actual NSE payload was captured and verified.

Example payload:

```json
[
 {
  "buyValue":"13553.36",
  "category":"DII",

  "category":"FII/FPI",
  "date":"16-Jun-2026",
  "netValue":"-749.18",

]
```

The architecture was then built around this verified structure.

# 64. FEATURE STORE PHILOSOPHY

---

# 65. THE BIGGEST RISK: LOOK-AHEAD BIAS

Institutional data is published after market close.

This creates a dangerous trap.

Example:

Monday flows become available Monday evening.

A model must never use:

Monday evening information

to predict:

Monday market behavior.

That would be cheating.

---

# 66. CHRONOLOGY HARDENING

The architecture enforces:

Strict T-1 Logic.

Meaning:

Prediction for Day T

may only use

Institutional data from Day T-1.

This is mandatory.

---

# 67. SHIFTED FEATURE STORE

A dedicated API exists:

get_shifted_features_for_inference()

Purpose:

Prevent accidental leakage.

The API physically shifts the feature matrix forward.

Example:

Tuesday Prediction

receives

Monday Flows.

Monday Prediction

receives

Friday Flows.

This guarantees chronology safety.

---

# 68. UNIT TEST VERIFICATION

Chronology protection is not merely documented.

It is tested.

A dedicated pytest unit test verifies:

Friday → Monday mapping.

Monday → Tuesday mapping.

No same-day leakage.

The test passes successfully.

This makes chronology protection verifiable rather than theoretical.

---

# 69. WHY THIS IS IMPORTANT

Many financial research projects fail because of hidden look-ahead bias.

A model appears brilliant.

Then fails in production.

The chronology hardening architecture exists specifically to prevent this.
