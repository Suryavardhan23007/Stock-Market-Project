# Options Intelligence (OI) Layer

## 1. Overview and Core Philosophy
The Options Intelligence (OI) Layer is a high-frequency, real-time data ingestion and analytics subsystem designed to monitor the Indian derivatives market (NIFTY 50 and BANKNIFTY). 

It is important to understand what this layer is **not**: It is not a price prediction engine. It does not predict the market using classical technical analysis (like RSI or MACD).

Instead, it is a **Market Structure Engine**. It monitors exactly how institutions, market makers, and option writers are positioning their capital. By observing changes in Open Interest (OI), options premiums, and volume at a granular level (1.5 seconds), the OI layer calculates "hidden" market forces such as:
- **Dealer Hedging Pressure (Gamma Exposure):** How options dealers are forced to buy or sell the underlying asset to remain delta-neutral.
- **Institutional Conviction:** Whether rising prices are fueled by genuine "Long Buildups" or fragile "Short Covering".
- **Structural Constraints:** Identifying heavy Put Walls (Support) or Call Walls (Resistance) where massive institutional capital is deployed to defend a price level.

**The ultimate goal of this layer is to convert raw option chain data into machine-learning-ready features.**

## 3. The "Raw Preservation" Strategy
The most critical architectural decision made in this layer is the **Raw Preservation Strategy**. 

When the system polls the broker (5paisa V2 API), it receives a JSON payload containing exact metrics for every option strike (e.g., Strike 21000 Call Option Premium, Open Interest, Volume, etc.). 

Instead of saving calculated analytics (like PCR or Gamma) directly to the database and discarding the raw data, the system saves the **exact, unmodified broker payload** into the PostgreSQL `options_raw_chain` table. 

**Why?**
1. **Zero Information Loss:** By saving the exact `ScripCode`, `Premium`, and `ChangeInOI`, the database becomes a perfect mirror of the exchange's order-matching engine. 
2. **Future-Proofing:** If we discover a bug in the Gamma calculation algorithm 6 months from now, or if we invent a new feature, we can retroactively calculate it perfectly because the underlying atomic raw data is perfectly preserved.
