# FII/DII Intelligence Layer - Version 2 Roadmap

This document outlines the advanced Feature Engineering methodologies scheduled for Version 2.
**CRITICAL RULE:** Do NOT implement these features until a pristine, continuous 30-60 day historical dataset has been accumulated in Version 1.

---

## 1. FII Flow Z-Score
**Purpose:** Identify statistically abnormal institutional activity.
**Formula:** `(Current FII Net Flow - 20_day_rolling_mean) / 20_day_rolling_std`
**Rationale:** A 5,000 Crore buy in a quiet market is massive. A 5,000 Crore buy during an earnings season might be average. Z-Scores normalize the flow relative to the current volatility regime.
**Expected Benefit:** Allows ML models to detect "Shock" injections of capital regardless of the underlying index value.

## 2. FII Percentile Framework
**Purpose:** Measure the extremity of current institutional participation over a very long horizon (e.g. 252 trading days).
**Formula:** Rank current day's flow against all historical flows, converting to a 0-100 score.
**Rationale:** Detects when FIIs are historically bearish (e.g., 5th percentile) or historically bullish (e.g., 99th percentile).
**Expected Benefit:** Reversion strategies. When percentiles hit 99%, the market often exhausts its buyers, signaling an imminent top.

## 3. Flow Velocity
**Purpose:** Measure the speed (acceleration/deceleration) of institutional participation.
**Formula:** `Current_Day_FII_Net - Previous_Day_FII_Net` (Δ FII Net Flow)
**Rationale:** If FIIs bought 1,000Cr yesterday and 5,000Cr today, velocity is highly positive. Capital deployment is accelerating.
**Expected Benefit:** Detects urgency. Urgent capital deployment precedes sharp momentum trends.

## 4. Flow Acceleration
**Purpose:** The second derivative of institutional participation.
**Formula:** `Current_Velocity - Previous_Velocity`
**Rationale:** Measures whether the rate of buying/selling is plateauing. 
**Expected Benefit:** Early detection of trend exhaustion before the actual net flow turns negative.

## 5. Institutional Regime Engine
**Purpose:** Classify the broader macro stance of the cash market.
**Logic Framework:**
- *Aggressive Accumulation:* FII > 0 AND DII > 0 (Both buying).
- *Aggressive Distribution:* FII < 0 AND DII < 0 (Both dumping).
- *Divergence (Foreign Led):* FII > 0 AND DII < 0.
- *Divergence (Domestic Led):* DII > 0 AND FII < 0.
**Expected Use:** Downstream ML models will branch their logic trees (e.g., Random Forests) based on the current regime (e.g., "If Aggressive Distribution, disregard bullish OI signals").

## 6. Multi-Horizon Rolling Flow Models
**Purpose:** Expand the current 3/5/10/20 day flows into deeper macro aggregations.
**Horizons:** 60-day, 120-day, 252-day.
**Expected Use:** Long-term index trajectory forecasting. FII cash flows over a 120-day window heavily dictate the baseline trend of the NIFTY 50.

## 7. OI + FII/DII Fusion Concepts (Fusion Layer)
**Purpose:** Combine Intraday Derivative positioning (OI Layer) with EOD Cash deployments (FII/DII Layer).
**Examples:**
- *Bullish Conviction:* Put Walls building in OI + FII Cash Accumulation = High probability of structural rally.
- *Bearish Conviction:* Call Walls building in OI + FII Cash Distribution = High probability of structural crash.
- *Trap Divergence:* Heavy Retail Put Buying in OI + FII Cash Accumulation = Market Makers squeeze retail shorts resulting in an explosive short-covering rally.
**Expected Use:** This will form the core logic of the ultimate `fusion_layer`.
